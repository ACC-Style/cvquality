Molecules consist of one or more nested rules, but each of them must not be more than an atom itself. For example, using icons or buttons in molecules is valid, but using other molecules is not.
To mark an element as molecule, annotate it with the @molecule tag, followed by the name of the component.

What is Quality Improvement for Institutions?

Quality Improvement for Institutions is a suite of registries, quality campaigns, accreditation services and clinical toolkits offered to health care facilities and their staff in a convenient, single website.  These online assets are intended to inspire and accelerate quality improvement in the facility while saving staff time and energy by providing ready-to-go, off-the-shelf programs and tools.

How do I access Quality Improvement for Institutions online?
The website for Quality Improvement for Institutions is CVQuality.ACC.org. 
Who is eligible to participate in Quality Improvement for Institutions?

Individuals from NCDR Registry participating hospitals have access to the program as part of their NCDR fee.

Individuals from participating PINNACLE Registry practices have access to the program as part of their PINNACLE Registry association.

Hospitals that do not participate in an NCDR hospital registry or PINNACLE Registry can join Quality Improvement for Institutions for an annual fee.

*At this time, hospitals accredited through ACC Accreditation services do not have access to the quality improvement resources available on CVQuality.ACC.org, unless they are also part of an NCDR registry. The ACC is working to provide access to ACC Accredited facilities.
